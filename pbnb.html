Product design case study
An app that keeps track of your personal plant care

DEMO VIDEO

The world of house plants is vast and deep rooted, like many hobbies, the experience levels of plant lovers range from casual hobbyiests to life-long cultivators. While house plants’ popularity has dramatically increased during the pandemic, along with numerous tech solutions to better help beginners maintain their indoor jungles, some more experienced plant parents are still using more traditional tools like a spreadsheet or notebooks (if they use documentation tools at all). Plantbot’s Notebook aims to be an easy tool for plant parents with more simple and hands-off experience to document and track their plants.

GOAL

To create a mobile app with a companion responsive website for plant parents and their plant care.

FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

  • onboarding & login/sign up
  • overview of user's calendar
  • overview of user's plant collection
  • plant specific data entry flow
  • map view & list of local plant shops, with shop invnetory view
  • inventory entry flow for plant shop owners

TARGET AUDIENCE

The target demographic is users who well versed in technology applications and have a strong affinity towards growing plants. The user has already made a commitment to the well-being of their plants, and is willing to formally record their collection. The age of user is less of a factor, as anyone can start collecting houseplants or cultivating a garden.

RISKS & OPPORTUNITIES

The risk is that users who are invested enough in their plants to use an app to track the care is a niche group, they likely have a system that works for them, and they may be too invested in their current system to switch to a new one. So the target audience of plant enthusiasts is not quite as abundant.
However, with the rising trend of Millenials and Gen Z in their quests for their urban forest, there is a growing number of new members in the plant community. Thanks to the plant community being very open to sharing, plants and assistance, once there is a good product available, it will spread within the community.

TOOLS USED

Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator, UsabilityHub, Optimal Sort, physical sketches

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

House plants are typically grown away from their native environments, plant parents mimick their specific habitats to keep the plants thriving.
The current plant app market is geared towards beginner plant keepers with a lot of handholding and prescriptive guidance, which can be inaccurate due to the varieties of factors that contributes to an individual plant’s health. It can be overwhelming when the plant collection is large and/or diverse for the plant parents to keep track of all these elements.
We will know the product is successful when we see users report less mental burden with their plant care, and also an improvement in their plants health.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

An analysis of a few existing plant related apps were made to gauge their functionality and design, and where there is room for improvement.

USER INTERVIEW

As a moderator of a rapidly growing online plant community, I see and talk to many plant parents about the different kinds of conditions and problems they encounter. However, I conducted 3 more in-dept interviews with members of the community that I think represents the spectrum of members, and also users that I am designing for.
The goal of the interviews is to understand the habits people have around their plant care, where are some areas that causes stress, how they document their plants, and their plant buying preferences.

As with any hobby after a certain level of investment, there is a lot of emotional attachment as well.
The continual responsibility of the plants as they grow and the sentimental value makes this a long-term hobby.
People tend to get more involved and emotionally and financially invested as time goes by.

USER PERSONA

Consolidating the information from the interviews and my general observation interacting with various plant parents, I created 3 personas, each representing different levels of plant-keeping experience, from beginner to intermediate to advanced. While my target audience is the intermediate level of plant parents, I thought it was important to keep the full spectrum of experience in mind, as users’ experience levels fluctuate with their involvement with the hobby.

USER JOURNEY

Two user journeys were created for the personas (Master Marsha serves as a reminder for easily understandable functionalities and UI) to explore their needs and emotional experience during some plant related activities, to assess where and how Plantbot’s Notebook can help them better achieve their goals.

USER FLOW

Finding areas from the User Journeys that can be improved, 3 user flows were created to map out the basic functions of some features. This helped me focus on the areas that can be within the product’s control.

LESSONS LEARNED

OVERALL EXPERIENCE

As my first time designing a digital product, this opened my eyes to all of the intricate details involved in product design, from the detailed and intensive research in the beginning, to the various considerations during the design process, and all of the transitions between screens.

RESEARCH

The amount of planning and prep work involved in the research process was very enlightening, I had to make sure I kept an open mind and be objective when analyzing the results, and not just rely on my personal experience or preconceptions. I have a new found appreciation for the process and the art of asking questions to not influence participants towards a certain direction.

TESTING

I learned the most during the usability tests, to see in real time testers’ reactions and thoughts and evaluate how well I was able to communicate the intent through design. It was also a test for my people-pleasing self to be critical about the feedback and evaluate which ones were important to implement, and which ones can wait until the next round of design update. Reviewing the original personas I created was helpful in prioritizing certain changes.

FURTHER ITERATIONS

There are still a lot of gaps in the design for this to be a fully functional product. The specific flow around Bulk Plant Care still needs refining, in terms of list view after the records are updated, and plants that may need multiple care actions. The current onbaording was not very clear to a lot of the testers, it needs a refresh as well as adding coachmarks once past the overview screens.
I would like to conduct testing with a more refined and robust prototype, mostly to test out some of the more repetitive tasks that can more accurately test out the tasks. I would also like for the testers to use the apps for a few weeks, for Plantbot to learn their plant’s needs, and get feedback on Plantbot’s training process and the reliability of the feature.
